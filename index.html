About ETC 599 ‚Äì AI for Master Learners

ETC 599 is a hands-on, project-based course from Northern Arizona University that helps learners build real AI literacy. Grounded in functional, ethical, rhetorical, and practical literacies, the course uses a Human-in-the-Loop approach so people remain in charge while AI supports thinking, creativity, and productivity.

Whether you're writing a paper, planning a lesson, leading a team, running a small business, or supporting your child's homework, this course gives you the mindset and skills to use AI‚Äîand question it‚Äîresponsibly.

What You'll Learn

By the end of this class, you will be able to use AI and ChatGPT with confidence, caution, and creativity.

Understand AI in everyday life Identify what AI and generative tools like ChatGPT are, where they can genuinely help, and what their main opportunities, limits, and risks are across school, work, and home.
Use AI safely and ethically Apply core AI policies, ethics, and safety guidelines‚Äîincluding privacy, bias, sustainability, and academic integrity‚Äîusing a Human-in-the-Loop mindset.
Work smarter with AI tools Use practical AI tools to save time, organize information, generate ideas, and support collaboration and learning without outsourcing your judgment.
Think and prompt critically Write clear, effective prompts using techniques like vibe coding; evaluate AI outputs for accuracy, logic, quality, and ethical alignment; and adjust when the AI gets it wrong.
Support others & build simple agents Guide children, students, or colleagues in safe AI use, and design simple AI agents, chatbots, and automations that streamline routine tasks in classrooms, homes, or workplaces.

Course Modules

The course is structured as four connected, workshop-style modules that move from foundations to real-world implementation.

Module 1
Foundations & Functional AI Literacy
  • Key generative AI terminology and concepts.
  • What GenAI is, what it can and cannot do.
  • Human-in-the-Loop mindset for safe, shared control.
  • First-hand experiences with both the helpful and harmful sides of AI, including sustainability impact.
Module 2
Critical Thinking & Ethical Prompting
  • Analyzing AI ethical issues: bias, privacy, sustainability, and academic integrity.
  • Using a critical thinking framework to evaluate AI responses with people in the loop.
  • Applying rhetorical situation and rhetorical triangle to engineer prompts and critique AI tone, stance, and credibility.
Module 3
Theory-Informed AI for Learning & Work
  • Connecting learning and communication theories to purposeful AI integration.
  • Trying GenAI tools to design inclusive, engaging learning and work activities.
  • Balancing efficiency with deep thinking and human connection.
Module 4
Implementation, Guidance, Assessment & Automation
  • Strategies for guiding students, employees, or family members in responsible AI use.
  • Designing authentic assessment and monitoring in the age of AI.
  • Creating simple AI agents, chatbots, and automations that support daily tasks while protecting ethics and safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a technical or programming background?
No. The course is designed for everyday learners. Curiosity and a willingness to experiment are more important than technical skills.
Is this only for teachers?
No. While educators will find it especially relevant, the course is open to graduate learners, professionals, parents, and community members who want to use AI thoughtfully.
What if I'm cautious or nervous about AI?
That is exactly who this course is for. We take concerns seriously and focus on safety, ethics, and human-in-the-loop decision-making at every step.
Will I actually build something?
Yes. You will design prompts, create small AI-supported projects, and prototype simple agents or chatbots that fit your real context.
Can non-NAU learners participate?
Yes. Interested learners can explore enrolling as non-degree-seeking students through NAU Admissions.

Ready to become an AI-confident master learner?

If you want AI to support your life without taking it over, ETC 599 is designed for you. Learn to work with AI tools responsibly, creatively, and confidently‚Äîwhile keeping the human firmly in the loop.
